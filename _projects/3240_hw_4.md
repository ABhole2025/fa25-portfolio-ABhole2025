Reflection:

Problem set 4 was quite educational for me as helped apply the so far learned heat transfer principles to an extremely relevant technology - solar panels. By modeling the PV panel as a multilayered thermal resistance network, I learned how to account for solar irradiation being absorbed at different depths—specifically the 10% absorbed at the glass surface versus the 85% converted in the silicon layer.This helped me think about solar panels in a more quantitative and scientific manner and helped me mentally link the efficiency of heat transfer in the whole process to the generational efficiency of the solar panel.

The idea of thermal resistance networks was explored in depth and I appreciated this a lot because it shows a more realistic model of heat transfer compare to the idealized ones I had enountered previously. This helps me see much more clearly where and how these principles apply to real machines and systems.
